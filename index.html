About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at University of Colorado Boulder in Information Science, under the supervision of Dr. Leysia Palen. I am expected to defend my dissertation in 2025. I earned my Master of Science in Information Science, in 2021. I also received my Bachelor of Science in Business Analytics and Entrepreneurship & Strategy with a minor in Information Science 2019 at Leeds School of Business.

My research broadly focuses on public health and social media; my current aim is to understand the mis- and disinformation phenomena happening in the area of vaccine acceptance and/or attitudes in vulnerable populations in sociotechnical systems. I use mixed methods to examine the shared information on social media platforms and the information seeking behaviors that happen. Previously, alongside my co authors in the PalenLab, my recent research project focuses on vaccine mis- and disinformation in online spaces that targets women and other marginalized communitie’s reproductive health.

Vacchine Behavior Change

One of the current research projects that I am working is understand the role of information and online platforms of ex-hesitant and ex-anti-vax individuals. in their acceptance of vaccines. This research uses mixed-methods to undertand the role of online community in behavior change, as well to identify specific information communication and framing that contributed to participant behavior change.

Vaccine Disinformation in Reproductive Health

One of my the project that I have been a part of is a multi-staged, multi method paper that studies mis/disinformation on social media, looking at vaccines and menstruation. It shows how medical silence on the topic of the side effects of the vaccine allowed mis/disinformation into the scene while creating uncertainty for others.
