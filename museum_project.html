OVERVIEW

This research project focuses on understand what makes a space a place. For this we used ethnography to observe the people that were going around, we did spontanous interviews with people to better understand their experence. We had two main research questions:

  • Research Question One: What makes the museum space a place?
  • Research Question Two: How is this space informated?


    • Project Details

    My Role: UX Research | Team Member
    Duration: 2 months
    Tools: Miro
    Skills: Observations, Interviews, Affinity Mapping

    PHASE 1 - UNDERSTAND

    Background Research We treated as if the Denver Musuem of Nature of Science as an client and performed background research to better undertand theer mission, values, and core values to better help us understand the business and to give recommendations.

    Interviews We performed spontaouns interviews with the cusotmers their to conduct a quick interview to better undertand their experiences, their likes and pain points.

    Research Plan: Three Stage Evolution We focused on zones that had interactions and would be collbrative, learning zones. The zones were as choosen: Our team decided on 5 different zones for 30 minutes with ratating one where there are two researchers. We made sure that the zones had different diverse locations with different lefvels of interactions. As at team we did a round of observation in an exhibit at the museum. We decided to have a group observation and data collection and focus on major exhibits. We focused on whrer do people with tickets go frist and how they are navigating? Compared to people with museum passes - how are they navigating the space? We sepnd time in informatics and walking through the space again-- paying attention to how to improve it.

    PHASE 2 - ANALYSIS

    As a team we collected the data from different zones and did a raw data dumpled on Miro. We had all different types data collected such as photos, fieldntoes and the museum map. We used multuple iterations of affinity mapping to find themes that answered resarch questions. We first came up with top level themes and them sub themes within the top level themes.

    PHASE 3 - Recommendations



    Short-Term Reccomendation

  • Child Care Assistance → Addressing shopping challenges for parents
  • Wayfinding Improvements → Enhancing in-store navigation
  • Chatbot Support → Streamlining online grocery assistance

    • Long-Term Reccomendation

  • Smart Fridge Integration → Automating grocery management
  • Smart Cart Technology → Enhancing in-store shopping efficiency

    • Reflections & Takeaways

    This project gave me the opportunity to collaborate with a diverse team, each member bringing unique perspectives. I learned the importance of active listening, open-mindedness, and trust in collaborative environments

  • Modification of Plans: When we were on the site, as a team we had to make decisions to better manage time and had to adjust our plan on the spot to make the study fit into our scehudles and to have sufficient time to complete our study.
  • Selecting the Right Methods: Choosing the appropriate research method is critical, especially when working within project timelines. This experience reinforced the importance of having a strong methodological foundation to make informed decisions.
  • Time Management & Teamwork: Working with a diverse team helped me appreciate different work styles and communication approaches. Understanding and adapting to these differences improved collaboration and efficiency.
