Museum Visitor Experience Recommendations

OVERVIEW

This research project explores what transforms a space into a place by examining visitor interactions within the Denver Museum of Nature & Science. Using ethnographic methods, we conducted observations and spontaneous interviews to understand visitor experiences and engagement.
We focused on two key research questions:

  • What elements make the museum space feel like a place?
  • How is this space informated?


    • Project Details

    My Role: UX Research | Team Member
    Duration: 2 months
    Tools: Miro
    Skills: Observations, Interviews, Affinity Mapping

    PHASE 1 - UNDERSTANDING THE EXPERIENCE

    Background Research We approached the Denver Museum of Nature & Science as if it were a client, analyzing its mission, values, and visitor engagement strategies. This helped us align our research with the museum’s objectives and frame actionable recommendations.

    Interviews & Observations To capture real visitor experiences, we conducted spontaneous interviews with museum attendees, gathering insights on their expectations, navigation challenges, and engagement levels.

    Research Plan: Three Stage Evolution To understand visitor interactions and wayfinding behaviors, we conducted observational research across five key museum zones. These zones were selected for their varying levels of engagement and collaboration, ensuring a diverse range of visitor experiences.

    1️⃣ Rotational Observations – Researchers rotated across the five zones every 30 minutes, with one zone always having two researchers to capture deeper insights.
    2️⃣ Visitor Navigation Study – We examined how different visitor groups—ticket holders vs. museum members—navigated the space, identifying common pathways and decision points.
    3️⃣ Information Systems Analysis – We assessed the effectiveness of wayfinding tools, exhibit layouts, and digital vs. analog information displays, noting areas for improvement.

    This structured approach allowed us to uncover patterns in visitor behavior, spatial navigation, and engagement, forming the basis for our design recommendations.

    PHASE 2 - ANALYSIS

    We synthesized our data using Miro, where we compiled:
    ✅ Photos and field notes
    ✅ Museum maps
    ✅ Interview transcripts

    We conducted multiple iterations of affinity mapping to cluster insights into top-level themes and sub-themes. This helped us distill complex qualitative data into clear behavioral patterns and pain points. To refine our recommendations, we asked:
      1) What should be added?
      2)What should be subracted?
      3) What should be converted?
      4) What works so well that it can inform a general design principle?

    Through team discussions and voting, we identified key experience gaps and opportunities for improvement.

    PHASE 3 - RECOMMENDATIONS

    Reccomendation #1: Aligning Exhibits with Museum Values

    Some exhibits do not fully align with the museum’s mission and core values. We recommend:

  • Archiving, replacing, or exchanging misaligned exhibits with value-driven displays
  • Collaborating with other museums to trade exhibits that better align with visitor interests.


    • Reccomendation #2: Enhancing Accessibility & Inclusivity

    Making exhibits more engaging and understandable for diverse audiences by:

  • Providing multilingual support in interactive and instructional content.
  • Designing child-friendly information by incorporating animations instead of text-heavy instructions.
  • Ensuring exhibits accommodate all age groups by varying content complexity and delivery methods.


    • Reccomendation #3: Improving Wayfinding & Navigation

    Clear and intuitive navigation enhances the visitor experience. We recommend:

  • Installing easy-to-read maps at museum entrances and inside individual exhibits.
  • Enhancing signage with visual cues and color-coded pathways for better navigation.


    • Reccomendation #4:Leveraging Long-Term Volunteers

    Museum volunteers often have valuable insights into visitor behavior. We suggest:

  • Gathering structured feedback from volunteers to identify recurring visitor pain points.
  • Incorporating volunteer suggestions into future museum experience improvements.


    • Reccomendation #5: Balancing Digital & Analog Learning

    A mix of traditional and modern learning methods can enhance engagement. We propose:

  • Introducing high-tech interactive elements (e.g., augmented reality, touchscreens) to enrich the visitor experience.
  • Ensuring digital tools complement rather than replace analog exhibits to maintain accessibility for all visitors.
